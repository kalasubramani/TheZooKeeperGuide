Zoo Keeper's Guide

Note: This website provides an insight into the personal characteristics of each of our zoo inhabitants. This page does not provide details about the diet plans or cage/habitat management for any of the zoo inhabitants. You can reach out to your Supervisor to obtain those details.

As a zoo keeper intern, it is essential for you to identify each inhabitant and understand their needs here and personal behaviors.

Notes to Zoo Keeper Interns

** Click on the images of the zoo inhabitants to further reading about their natural habitat,distribution across the globe and general knowledge facts.

Bears

Ollie

Ollie is a calm and friendly girl who loves to spend time playing around in the Zoo's natural setting. She loves fish and honey. She is an adorable person who loves petting.

Mona

Mona is the naughty girl of the zoo. She loves to climb and run around. She loves blackberries. She prefers to be on her own.

Giraffes

  1. Frankie

    Frankie is social and easygoing. He is a greedy guzzler. He can guzzle up to 45kg of leaves and twigs a day!

  2. Coconut

    Coconut is rare white giraffe. Poachers kill rare white giraffes. He is a very precious for our zoo. He is shy and hides out from view most of the time.

Lions

  1. Mella

    Mella is the big mommy in our zoo. She just gave birth to only one cub this time. So she has been very protective and defensive lately. Only senior zoo keepers are allowed to handle her.

  2. Karl

    Karl is rare white Lion. The white lions are held in high esteem by the local tribes in Timbavati, South Africa. Due to the rarity of the white lion, many were brought into captive breeding. Karl is one such cub who was brought here from Timbavati Private Nature Reserve. He is the youngest one in our zoo. He is just three months old.

Monkeys

  1. Cookie

    This little guy is very curious and social, and you can often find him carefully watching staff when they enter the exhibit. He love scent marking and frequently rub himselves all over anything new to show ‘ownership’, even of the keepers, who he mark with tiny hugs! He usually makes his way onto his keeper’s shoulder when it comes time to snack !

  2. Earl

    Earl belongs to probosis monkey family. Proboscis Monkeys look Too Human. He frequently leaps from tree limbs and hits the water with a comical belly flop. He is should not be fed bananas at any cost. Bananas are fatal to probosis monkeys.

  3. Banana Pudding

    Banana Pudding is known for his inquisitiveness and intelligence. He is incredibly sociable, ofcourse the sweetheart of all zoo keepers here. He easily solves a rubix cube.

Alligators

  1. Wren

    Wren do not prefer humans. He can deliver a nasty bite in self-defense if within his vicinity. Only senior zoo keepers are allowed to enter his area.

  2. Aspen

    Aspen is an unusual and entertaining pet. However, he can bite when they feel threatened or in danger. You must hold on getting next to him until you become a reptile expert.

  3. Mika

    Mika is the big mommy of our zoo. She is close to 15feet long. She very shy and will typically hide from humans. She loves fish. Her species is Critically Endangered. She must be monitored regularly and handled with utmost care!
